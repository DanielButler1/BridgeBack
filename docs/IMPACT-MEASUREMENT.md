# Impact measurement framework

## Product hypothesis

If a teacher can start with the upcoming lesson and review an accurate, source-grounded prerequisite map, then BridgeBack may reduce repetitive preparation while giving a returning pupil a more manageable route into classroom participation.

## Leading measures

| Measure | Definition | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Draft preparation time | Time from submitting sources to a reviewable map | Measures system latency, not workload impact |
| Teacher review time | Time spent correcting and approving the map | The primary workflow-effort measure |
| Map edit rate | Concepts or edges added, removed or changed | Indicates draft usefulness |
| Source fidelity | Proportion of references supported by supplied material | Prevents invented curriculum evidence |
| Pathway size | Number and duration of assigned activities | Tests the manageable-route promise |
| Help requests | Pupil requests for teacher support during an activity | Supports human escalation without treating it as failure |
| Stopping and resuming | Sessions paused and resumed without lost progress | Measures pupil agency and product reliability |

## Outcome measures for a future supervised pilot

- Teacher-recorded preparation time compared with the school's existing process.
- Teacher judgment of whether the pupil could participate in the target lesson.
- Pupil-reported clarity, manageability and control.
- Percentage of routes completed, paused or declined.
- Accessibility and safeguarding incidents.

## Guardrails

- No use in grades, discipline, admissions, setting or attendance action.
- No ranking pupils or comparing pathway completion.
- No inference about absence reasons, health, disability, emotion or motivation.
- No claim that pathway completion proves readiness or mastery.
- No pupil is required to finish BridgeBack before entering a lesson.

## Internal workload benchmark

Before a school pilot, run the same five synthetic lesson packs through two documented processes:

1. Manually identify prerequisites, write four questions and prepare up to three short activities.
2. Upload the pack to BridgeBack, review every source and concept, correct the draft and approve the route.

Record active time, corrections and errors. This is a product benchmark, not teacher validation. Report the raw timings and method rather than claiming general workload savings.

## AI-simulated teacher walkthrough

During Build Week, a separate model agent may inspect the synthetic teacher and pupil journeys using the role and constraints of a UK secondary teacher. Record this only as an **AI-simulated, role-based heuristic evaluation**. It can identify unclear controls, likely workflow friction, educational inconsistencies and safeguarding questions; it is not teacher validation, user research, expert endorsement or evidence of workload reduction.

The first walkthrough identified two material Mathematics issues that were corrected: the demo described a check-in without offering one, and the dependency map omitted solving linear equations while treating checking a solution as a prerequisite. These findings are useful design evidence, not claims about real educators.

## Minimum real educator check

Before claiming educator validation, ask at least three practising teachers, tutors, PGCE trainees or former teachers to complete the same 15–20 minute synthetic task. Record their role accurately and measure:

- time from opening the lesson to approving or rejecting the map;
- concepts and dependency edges they add, remove or change;
- source references they cannot verify;
- confidence that the route is small enough and sufficient for participation;
- the answer to: “What would stop you using this on Monday?”

Report the participant count, recruitment method and raw results. Do not generalise beyond that sample.
