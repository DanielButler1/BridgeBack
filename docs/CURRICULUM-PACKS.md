# Curriculum packs

BridgeBack uses curriculum specifications as a navigation layer. They help a teacher identify the lesson a pupil needs to rejoin and give the prerequisite analysis a stable reference point. They do not replace a school's scheme of work, lesson materials or teacher judgement.

The topic codes and short titles identify the relevant specification areas. The summaries and prerequisite relationships are original BridgeBack metadata, designed to support lesson selection and the first draft of a dependency map. They must be reviewed alongside the teacher's uploaded materials before pupil work is assigned.

## Product behaviour

1. A teacher can connect a new class to a supported curriculum pack or choose a custom curriculum.
2. A curriculum-linked class offers grouped specification topics when the teacher creates the upcoming lesson.
3. Choosing a topic fills a plain-language starting objective and shows likely earlier knowledge.
4. The selected pack and topic codes are saved on the class and lesson in Convex.
5. Lesson objectives and uploaded school resources remain the source used for AI analysis. Curriculum metadata is context, not evidence that a concept was taught.

## Versioning and review

- Pack keys contain the awarding body, specification code and represented version, for example `ocr-j277-v3.1`.
- Existing classes retain the pack version they selected. A future specification update should be added as a new pack rather than silently changing an existing route.
- Sources and represented versions should be checked before each public release and at least once per academic year.
- BridgeBack is not affiliated with or endorsed by OCR. Schools remain responsible for checking that their curriculum and assessment materials are current.

## Copyright boundary

BridgeBack stores the minimum factual taxonomy needed to identify curriculum areas. It does not reproduce the full specification, teaching guidance, assessment material or copyrighted lesson content. The interface links back to OCR's primary documents for authoritative detail.
