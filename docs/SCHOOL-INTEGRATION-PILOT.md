# School integration and pilot plan

BridgeBack is not approved for identifiable pupil data. This plan describes the evidence and governance required before that boundary changes.

## Product proposition

BridgeBack is a next-lesson readiness tool for pupils returning after absence. It does not rebuild every missed lesson. It works backwards from the upcoming lesson, identifies the source-supported prerequisites, checks the pupil against those prerequisites and prepares no more than three focused activities.

The safe current claim is:

> BridgeBack is designed to reduce the repetitive preparation involved in turning lesson materials into focused support.

BridgeBack does not yet claim to reduce workload, improve attendance, improve attainment, reduce anxiety or prove that a pupil is ready for a lesson.

## Stage 0: product preparation

- Complete the production lesson and source-analysis workflow.
- Run accessibility checks at desktop and phone sizes.
- Test curriculum quality across at least two subjects.
- Maintain synthetic identities and lesson records.
- Prepare a child-specific DPIA template, retention schedule, acceptable-use policy and incident process.

## Stage 1: synthetic school review

Use five to ten fictional absence scenarios across Computer Science and one contrasting subject. Reviewers complete the same task: select the upcoming lesson, add sources, inspect the draft map, correct it and approve it.

Measure:

- time from lesson selection to approved map;
- concepts added, removed, relinked or reworded;
- unsupported source references;
- failed or blocked generations;
- confusing or unnecessary teacher actions;
- keyboard, screen-reader and phone usability.

This stage measures product reliability. It is not teacher or pupil impact evidence.

## Stage 2: school shadow mode

After privacy, safeguarding and contractual approval, work with one department and two teachers. BridgeBack prepares pathways from historical, fictional or otherwise approved cases, but no pupil receives generated support.

Compare the time needed to prepare an equivalent route manually with the time needed to review and correct BridgeBack's draft. Record the comparison rather than asking for a general estimate.

## Stage 3: supervised pupil pilot

- One subject and approximately 10–20 pupils over four to six weeks.
- School-managed identities and minimum necessary records.
- Teacher approval before every pathway.
- An always-visible route to ask a teacher for help.
- The pathway remains optional and never controls access to classroom teaching.
- No absence reason, health, behaviour or safeguarding information is entered.

Measure:

- median teacher preparation and review time;
- maps accepted, lightly edited or rejected;
- unsupported concepts or sources that reach review;
- pupil completion and stopping rates;
- pupil-reported clarity, manageability and agency;
- teacher judgment of participation in the target lesson;
- accessibility problems and safeguarding incidents.

Example pilot targets may be defined before the pilot, but they must never be presented as achieved results.

## Integration sequence

1. Manual lesson upload and synthetic CSV roster testing.
2. Microsoft or Google single sign-on.
3. Read-only imports from Teams, OneDrive, Google Classroom or Drive.
4. Optional roster synchronisation with the school's approved MIS provider.
5. Export a short support summary into the teacher's existing workflow.

Early pilots should not write AI-generated pupil judgments back into an MIS.

## Claims requiring real evidence

Do not claim that BridgeBack reduces workload, improves attendance or attainment, reduces anxiety, identifies mastery, prevents pupils falling behind, works equally across subjects or SEND profiles, or has been validated by teachers until suitable evidence exists.
